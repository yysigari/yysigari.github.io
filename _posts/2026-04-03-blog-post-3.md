Iran War: Morally committed, but epistemically humble 
====
``I hold non-negotiable moral principles about dignity and autonomy, but I reject the reduction of complex societies into morally uniform categories.''

I am shaped by a country I both belong to and resist. My roots in Iran are deep, yet my lived experience placed me at odds with a system that demands conformity over autonomy. As someone unwilling to subordinate identity to imposed authority, I found exclusion not in the absence of connection, but in the refusal to relinquish selfhood.

My stance is grounded in the principle that no political or religious order can justifiably override individual dignity. While I reject the structures that constrained my freedom, I do not reduce a society to a single narrative; experiences within it remain plural and uneven.

I stand, therefore, in a position of dual recognition: critically opposed to coercive power, yet enduringly connected to the people and history from which I come. This tension is not a contradiction, but the condition of an honest moral life.
